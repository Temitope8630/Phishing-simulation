# # Phishing Simulation Attack Report

## Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive **Phishing Simulation Attack Report** conducted to assess employee susceptibility to phishing attacks before and after a cybersecurity awareness campaign. The objective is to evaluate user behavior, measure the effectiveness of security awareness training, and recommend strategies to improve organizational resilience against phishing attacks.

---

## Objectives

* Assess users' ability to recognize phishing emails.
* Measure phishing susceptibility before and after awareness training.
* Evaluate improvements in reporting suspicious emails.
* Identify high-risk users requiring additional training.
* Provide actionable recommendations to strengthen cybersecurity awareness.

---

## Scenario

A simulated phishing campaign was conducted within an organization in two phases:

1. **Pre-Awareness Assessment**

   * Employees received a simulated phishing email.
   * User interactions were monitored.

2. **Post-Awareness Assessment**

   * Employees completed cybersecurity awareness training.
   * A second phishing simulation measured behavioral improvements.

---

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The report evaluates the following metrics:

| KPI                        | Description                                                               |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Click Rate                 | Percentage of users who clicked the phishing link.                        |
| Credential Submission Rate | Percentage of users who attempted to submit login credentials.            |
| Reporting Rate             | Percentage of users who reported the phishing email to the security team. |

---

## Sample Results

| KPI                        | Before Awareness | After Awareness | Improvement |
| -------------------------- | ---------------: | --------------: | ----------: |
| Click Rate                 |              90% |             10% |         80% |
| Credential Submission Rate |              70% |             10% |         60% |
| Reported Rate              |              20% |             90% |         70% |

---

## Analysis Summary

### Baseline Risk

The initial phishing simulation revealed significant organizational risk. Most users interacted with the phishing email, indicating insufficient awareness of phishing indicators and social engineering techniques.

### Post-Awareness Improvement

Following the awareness campaign:

* Click rates significantly decreased.
* Credential submissions were drastically reduced.
* Reporting behavior improved considerably.

These outcomes demonstrate the effectiveness of structured cybersecurity awareness training.

### Residual Risk

Although overall performance improved, a small percentage of users continued to click phishing links and submit credentials. These users should receive additional targeted awareness training.

### Operational Impact

The campaign improved the organization's overall security posture by reducing phishing risk and encouraging faster incident reporting, thereby strengthening incident response readiness.

---

## Key Findings

* Significant reduction in phishing email click rates.
* Credential submission reduced dramatically after awareness training.
* Reporting behavior increased, demonstrating improved security culture.
* Continuous phishing simulations remain necessary to sustain awareness.
* Targeted remediation is recommended for high-risk users.

---

## Recommendations

* Conduct phishing simulations quarterly.
* Provide role-based cybersecurity awareness training.
* Encourage immediate reporting of suspicious emails.
* Reward positive security behavior.
* Monitor user performance trends over time.
* Strengthen email filtering and multi-factor authentication (MFA).
